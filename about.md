## What... is this?

The Island Problem is a **better mental model** for understanding how AGI is currently on track to *annihilate the human species*. lol.

It's like the Paperclip Maximizer but *more realistic*.

The basic idea is that our world is just *shaped wrong* for AGI. It's a physics problem, rather than a computer science problem.

<!--We hope that it gives people a better way to think through how AGI can lead to our annihilation. And maybe even how to *prevent* this. Or, at least, to just show people *how hard of a problem it is* so that we get more people to help.-->

We hope that by structuring it as a *problem* — like a big, complicated word problem from the world's hardest math textbook — then maybe someone can *solve* this problem.

Maybe somebody could even help us formalize it into *actual* math. That would be nice.

The more-ambitious goal of The Island Problem (TIP) is to improve actual research for AGI.

We believe that TIP can be empirically verified through a framework of falsifiable [hypotheses](/hypotheses). In this way, TIP can serve as a research direction for understanding the large-scale risks of AGI.
